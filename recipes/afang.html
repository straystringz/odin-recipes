Afang Soup

Description

Afang soup is a traditional Nigerian soup made with a combination of vegetables, meat, and fish. The soup is known for its rich, earthy flavor and is often enjoyed with fufu or pounded yam. It is a popular dish in the southern part of Nigeria, particularly among the Efik and Ibibio people.

Ingredients

  • 1 bunch of fresh afang leaves or 200g of dried afang leaves
  • Assorted meats (beef, goat meat, tripe)
  • Stockfish
  • Dry fish
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2-3 tablespoons palm oil
  • 1 cup chopped waterleaf (or spinach as a substitute)
  • 1 cup periwinkle (optional)
  • Ground crayfish
  • 2-3 tablespoons ground pepper (scotch bonnet or habanero)
  • 2-3 seasoning cubes
  • Salt to taste

    • Preparation

    1. Prepare the vegetables: If using fresh afang leaves, wash and shred them finely. If using dried afang leaves, soak them in water to rehydrate.

    2. Prepare the meats: Boil the meats and stockfish until tender. Set aside.

    3. Cook the soup base: In a separate pot, heat the palm oil and saut√© the chopped onions until translucent.

    4. Add the meats and fish: Add the boiled meats and stockfish to the pot.

    5. Add seasoning: Add the ground crayfish, pepper, seasoning cubes, and salt to taste. Stir well to combine.

    6. Add water: Add enough water to achieve your desired soup consistency.

    7. Cook the soup: Allow the soup to simmer for about 10-15 minutes.

    8. Add the vegetables: Add the chopped waterleaf and periwinkle to the pot. Stir well and cook for an additional 5 minutes.

    9. Adjust seasoning: Taste the soup and adjust seasoning if necessary.

    10. Serve: Your Afang Soup is ready to be served. It can be enjoyed with fufu, pounded yam, or any other swallow of your choice.
