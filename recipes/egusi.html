Ofe Egusi

Description

Egusi soup is a popular Nigerian dish made with ground melon seeds and a variety of other ingredients. It is known for its rich, nutty flavor and is often enjoyed with fufu, pounded yam, or other Nigerian staples. The soup can be prepared with a combination of meats, fish, and vegetables, making it a hearty and satisfying meal.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups egusi (ground melon seeds)
  • Assorted meats (beef, goat meat, tripe)
  • Stockfish
  • Dry fish
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2-3 tablespoons palm oil
  • 1 cup chopped vegetables (spinach, kale, or pumpkin leaves)
  • Ground crayfish
  • 2-3 tablespoons ground pepper (scotch bonnet or habanero)
  • 2-3 seasoning cubes
  • Salt to taste

    • Preparation

    1. Prepare the meats: Boil the meats and stockfish until tender. Set aside.

    2. Prepare the egusi: In a bowl, mix the ground egusi with a little water to form a paste. Set aside.

    3. Cook the soup: In a separate pot, heat the palm oil and saut√© the chopped onions until translucent.

    4. Add the egusi: Add the egusi paste to the pot and stir continuously to avoid lumps.

    5. Add the meats: Add the boiled meats and stockfish to the pot.

    6. Add seasoning: Add the ground crayfish, pepper, seasoning cubes, and salt to taste. Stir well to combine.

    7. Add water: Add enough water to achieve your desired soup consistency.

    8. Cook the soup: Allow the soup to simmer for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally.

    9. Add vegetables: Add the chopped vegetables to the pot and stir well. Cook for an additional 5 minutes.

    10. Adjust seasoning: Taste the soup and adjust seasoning if necessary.

    11. Serve: Your Egusi Soup is ready to be served. It can be enjoyed with fufu, pounded yam, or any other swallow of your choice.
